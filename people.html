The Journey

Meriwether Lewis and William Clark traveled across the Louisiana Territory. In search of a water route to the Pacific Ocean, Lewis and Clark traveled by boat, horse, and by foot. Horses, supplies, and directions were donated by Native Americans. Most of the goods were traded back and forth with different Native American tribes. One of these Native Americans that Lewis and Clark came across was Sacagawea. Looking for her lost brother, while carring a child(s), joined Lewis and Clark on there expedition.

Spyglass

A replicated version of a spyglass Lewis or Clark would carry. Used to spy on nature or any potential threat. The spyglass was a key factor in guiding Lewis, Clark, and their crew, while also assuring their safety. Documenting everything witnessed by Lewis or Clark was all captured in the glass eye of the ancient spyglass.
