Jefferson's Spectacles

Thomas Jefferson's spectacles were a surprisily a key factor toward the Louisiana Purchase. The spectacles witnessed Jefferson writing and signing the document of approval to Congress, regarding the purchase. The spectacles are a hidden gem in History that has a memory of a thousand words.

Lewis and Clark's Backpack

An everyday item such as a backpack was exceptionally useful for Lewis and Clark. They could carry all their possessions on their back instead of using their hand or arms. This allowed for more hand-held tools to be used such as a compass.

Sacagawea's Babypack

Sacagawea having a child was not only difficult for her but also for the crew. This led to the invention of her babypack. This was like a backpack, but for a baby. The babypack was extremely efficent by having the baby able to be carried on the back, instead of taking up hand and arm space.

Napoleon's Hate
