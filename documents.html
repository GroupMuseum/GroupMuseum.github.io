Documents

Emancipation Proclamation

A presidential proclamation issued by President Abraham Lincoln. This proclamation stated that all slaves are free. It also stated that African Americans were to be treated as regular people. The Emancipation Proclamation was required to be followed (a law).

Missouri Compromise

The Missouri Comprimise was an effort by the U.S. Senate and House of Representatives to maintain a balance of power between the slaveholding states and free states. The slaveholding states feared that if they became outnumbered in Congressional representation that they would lack the power to protect their interests in property and trade.

Three-Fifths Compromise

The population of slaves would be counted as three-fifths in total when apportioning Representatives, as well as Presidential electors and taxes. The Three-Fifths Compromise was proposed by James Wilson and Roger Sherman, who were both delegates for the Constitutional Convention of 1787.
