Abolitionists

William Lloyd Garrison

An abolitionist that owned a newspaper company; The Liberator, which spoke out against slavery. The Liberator became the most famous newspaper of its time.

Harriet Beecher Stowe

An abolitionist that achieved national fame for her anti-slavery novel, Uncle Tom's Cabin. This fanned the flames of sectionalism before the Civil War.

John Greenleaf Whittier

An Abolitionist who was also a poet. Whittier published the antislavery pamphlet Justice and Expediency. From there, Whittier dedicated the next twenty years of his life to the abolitionist cause.

Jonathan Walker

An abolitionist shipwright and sea captain from New England. Jonathan Walker became famous for the unusual punishment he received for aiding seven slaves to escape. Walker was imprisoned and chained to the cell floor. Given little food and water Jonathan perished. However, this didn't stop Walker from writing about his life in prison.
